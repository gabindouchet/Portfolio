About

Engineer first.
Strategist by choice.

I graduated in Mechanical Engineering from Concordia University, where I learned that every technical solution starts with a human need. That belief has shaped everything since — from leading a student team to build a single-seater vehicle, to a 6-month R&D internship at Europe's leading aerial work platforms manufacturer.

An exchange semester at the National University of Singapore pushed me further, showing me that cultural fluency is as strategic as technical skill. It sparked a deeper curiosity for lean management, business efficiency, and the systems that turn ideas into execution.

I'm now pursuing a Master in Management at ESSEC Business School to formalize that intersection — and build a career at the crossroads of engineering precision and business strategy, with a focus on consulting and operations.
